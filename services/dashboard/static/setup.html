Welcome.

Vision Labs is a multi-camera AI security stack — person and vehicle detection, face recognition, DVR recording, and a chat assistant that can answer questions about what's happening across your cameras.

This wizard will:

  • Detect your GPU and recommend a configuration
  • Help you add your first camera
  • Drop you into the dashboard

You can re-run this wizard later by deleting /data/setup-state/setup.json inside the dashboard container's auth-data volume.

Detect your hardware.

We'll ask the orchestrator to spawn a one-shot nvidia-smi probe so we can see what GPUs are available. This may take up to ~30 seconds on the first run while the probe image downloads.

Location & storage.

Pick the timezone the system should use for "today" and "yesterday" queries, and decide how long to keep recordings, snapshots, and AI clips on disk.

Used for all date queries (AI chat, event journals, retention cutoffs). Default America/Toronto. Pick the IANA zone that matches your physical location.
Reducing retention immediately purges older data on the next prune cycle. Increasing it has no effect on already-deleted files.

Add your first camera.

You can scan your network for ONVIF-compatible cameras (Reolink, Hikvision, Dahua, etc.), or enter an RTSP URL manually. Skip the step to add cameras later from the Cameras tab.

Connect to camera

Enter the camera's credentials. We'll use them once to fetch the RTSP URL via ONVIF.

Final check.

Quick sanity check before we drop you on the dashboard: we'll wait up to 30 seconds for a frame from each camera you added. If nothing shows up, the camera URL/credentials are likely wrong — fixable in the Cameras tab.

Connect Telegram alerts (optional)

With Telegram connected, your phone becomes a remote for the whole stack. From anywhere you have data you can:

  • Get instant snapshots + AI scene descriptions when a person, vehicle, or known face is detected
  • Pull a fresh live photo on demand: /snapshot
  • Grab a 5-40s video clip from any camera: /clip 15 driveway
  • Ask the AI assistant questions from the chat: /ask was anyone in the backyard tonight?
  • Check who's currently in frame: /who · arm/disarm alerts: /arm

Skip this step if you'd rather use the dashboard only — you can connect Telegram later from .env.

1. Create your bot

  1. Open Telegram (mobile or desktop)
  2. Search for @BotFather and start a chat
  3. Send /newbot
  4. Pick a name and username when prompted
  5. BotFather replies with a token like 123456:ABC... — copy it here

2. Send a message to your bot

Open Telegram and send any message (or just /start) to your bot: @yourbot

We'll wait up to 30 seconds for your message to arrive, then capture your chat ID so the bot knows where to send alerts.
